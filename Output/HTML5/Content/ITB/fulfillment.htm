What Is It?

CX Fulfillment Measure is a single dedicated measure focused on the completion of a sale, i.e., the delivery/pick-up experience for a store pickup or shipment to home, office, etc.

  • Delivery fulfillment - Measures satisfaction with the purchase process, delivery, and product after the purchase is received.
  • In-store fulfillment - Measures satisfaction with the online purchase, in-store pick-up experience after a period of time in which the pick-up should have occurred.
  • CX Fulfillment Measure is available in both web and mobile channels.

Target Audiences

Customer Experience, eCommerce, Fulfillment, and Customer Support teams

These teams want to understand the customer satisfaction with the purchase process and retrieval of a product. They want to know if the customer’s needs during this process are being met in order to foster future sales.

Actionable Insights

A national drugstore chain offered in-store pickup for its prescriptions. While their main business is selling prescription and over-the-counter drugs, they also sell an assortment of other miscellaneous consumer goods. As a result, our client wanted to see how much of their business was an up-sell stemming from an in-store pickup. ForeSee’s analysis of customer visits revealed of those who picked up their prescription, 39% purchased at least one additional item from the store. Of those who made a purchase, 3% said they would not have made that purchase had they not been in the store.

A national home improvement chain sold several of its products through their digital channels and shipped them to their customers. They noticed their customer satisfaction with delivery had been decreasing and were interested in understanding why. ForeSee’s measure revealed that the decrease in delivery satisfaction could be traced back to a distribution center that was not performing well since that particular distribution center was sending customers damaged products and mixing up orders causing erroneous shipments. ForeSee recommended improving the packaging processes within their distribution center to increase customer satisfaction and reduce returns and refunds.

What Else Do I Need To Know?

  • This product has no prerequisites or dependencies.
  • The client needs to make sure that the audience being targeted has exposure to all elements of the model. See Possible Fulfillment Flows.
    A person ordering flowers is not usually the person who is the recipient of the flowers.
  • Within deployment, either Foresee or the client can send the invitation. This can be accomplished either in the fulfillment confirmation email or as a separate email with just a link to the survey.
  • If the company doesn’t send confirmation emails then the timing for when the email with the link gets sent needs to be considered so the end user does not get the survey email before they get their purchase.

Common CQs & CPPs

Common CQs

  • Have all items been received?
  • Did delivery meet expectations? If not, why not?
  • Items delivery/quality/fit/etc verses expectations
  • Did you contact customer service? Was your issue resolved? (Leads to call center measurement)

Common CPPs

  • Distribution center
  • Products included
  • Number of shipments to completely fulfill the order
  • Carrier used
  • Location of delivery
